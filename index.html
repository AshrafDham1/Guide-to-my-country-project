United Arab Emirates Location

Situated in the Middle East and West Asia, bordering the Gulf of Oman and the Persian Gulf, between Oman and Saudi Arabia The UAE stretches for more than 650 km (400 mi) along the southern shore of the Persian Gulf, with a total area of 83,600 square kilometres.

Brief Uae history

Pearling industry becomes key to the economies of the region.
Pearling trade declines due to global economic depression and invention of the cultured pearl.
Onshore exploration for oil takes place
First commercial discoveries of oil are made, off Abu Dhabi, with oil exports starting in 1962
Britain announces it will withdraw its military forces from "East of Suez" and end its treaty with the seven trucial sheikhdoms by 1971.
After independence from Britain, Abu Dhabi, Ajman, Dubai, Fujayrah, Sharjah, and Umm al Quwain come together as the United Arab Emirates (UAE). Sheikh Zayed Bin-Sultan Al Nuhayyan presides over the federation.
UAE joins the Arab League and UN.
Ras al-Khaymah joins the federation.
UAE becomes a founding member of the Gulf Cooperation Council.
First-ever national elections. A small number of hand-picked voters choose half of the members of the Federal National Council - an advisory body.
